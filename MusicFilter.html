Absu are crazy. They started in the 90's as a doomy death metal band. Proscriptor McGovern joined, and they started becoming more thrashy and black metal-y. Not a Norwegian or European black metal, but an American version. It's very fast, very interesting, and very thrashy. Proscriptor is the drummer and vocalist. Proscriptor uses insane black metal vocals while drumming extremely fast and quite technical. Their lyrical themes are mainly around Celtic and Sumerian mythology, and magick and occultism. "Absu" in Sumerian mythology refers to a subterranean ocean. The one word I think of when I think of Absu is "aggressive".

I originally found Absu from YouTube back in 2009. I had not yet started delving into harsh vocals and black metal, but this band is one that opened that door for me. I was listening to a lot of Einherjer. I mainly liked their album "Odin Owns Ye All", which uses clean vocals. Their other albums, however, used black metal-type vocals. I listened to some of those EPs and albums, but I didn't yet open that harsh vocal door, completely. Well, back in '09, there was a video that kept popping up in my suggestion list. It was for Absu's music video "Manannan". The thumbnail was of the guitarist - it wasn't too interesting, but the name "Absu" and the song title intrigued me for some reason. That entire visual intrigued me, but for some reason I was apprehensive. Well, eventually my curious got the better of me, and I clicked the video! I suppose if you see a suggested video constantly, eventually you're going to click it lol.

I remember watching it late at night on my laptop while I was in bed. It was a music video, but I was so confused when I saw (who I think might be an actor?) with the facepaint and performing the vocals, but I also saw the drummer screaming the vocals in several shots. I liked it, but I was more fascinated with the band's image and the drummer's craziness than I was the music. I eventually listened to their most-famous record, "Tara", and a few songs completely captured me. After that, and as I slowly started devouring black metal and harsh vocals, I liked Absu more and more.

Archspire is a technical death metal band from Canada. They're characteristic of being one of the fastest bands out there and highly technical. On top of that, their vocalist is essentially a rapper. The band members listen to metal, but they also take influence from rappers. The vocalist uses harsh vocals, but he also uses exceptionally fast phrasing, which is essentially a speed-rapping cookie monster. Not only are all of the instruments machine gun fast, but the vocals are too! They're a very fun band full of humor and character.

Awards:

  • Juno Album of the Year - 2022 (won)

These guys became one of my favorite bands around 2019~. I had recently found Blood Incantation's album "Hidden History of the Human Race", which diverted me from a death metal interest toward technical death metal. Archspire had recently released an INCREDIBLE album, "Relentless Mutation", which blew me away. I think that one reason why I like their music is because it's exceptionally fast and technical, but they also have evident melody and memorable songs - the songs don't simply blend into each other like a lot of death metal bands. With such extreme music, that can definitely happen - you end up with a wall of sound that results in iconic moments, but not memorable *songs*.

I started liking this band so much that I created my own merch! They had a piece of merch which was a baseball jersey with their logo on it. I liked it a lot, and decided to make my own!

One reason why I became so interested in this band is because of one of their guitar players' YouTube channels, Dean Lamb. Like I mentioned before, the band has a lot of humor and character, and Dean certainly embodies that. I really liked their music before I found his channel, so I was already oriented towards this direction of metal, but also being exposed to their culture and personality undoubtedly shifted them to the forefront. They'd be at the forefront because of their music, regardless, but afterall, knowing the character of a band and its members is part of the allure. In fact, Dean and the vocalist Oli made a video where Oli shared his famous "Friends with Bennies" recipe. I learned how to make it, and it's awesome! Blood Incantation is a death metal band from Denver, Colorado, formed in 2011. They have an old school, groovy style, but they add in progressive and technical personality. Their lyrical themes often revolve around space and death. They're similar to Vastum and Spectral Voice (two members are also in Spectral Voice), but are extra technical. Their music is aggressive and groovy, but it's also interesting. They're a fairly trippy band who like to think about existential ideas like aliens and death, and that's reflected in their music. Their songs are generally longer (not always), and their albums seem to be best listened to from start to finish.

I found Blood Incantation after listening to the Vastum / Spectral Voice split EP. They had just released the album "Hidden History of the Human Race", so it was serendipitous that I found Vastum and Spectral Voice. I fell in love with that album and listened to it for a solid 3-4 weeks (I added in "Starspawn" eventually, as well). These 3 bands - Vastum, Spectral Voice, and Blood Incantation - really oriented me toward technical death metal; especially Blood Incantation. I already liked old school death metal - Dhwesha was a band that I was very very fond of - so these 3 very much-so captured me. They had a slightly trippy and more modern take to the OSDM vibe, which was great. I also very much like that their vocals and mix is very cavernous - it gives the music a depth and often facilitates a more intriguing listen, like they're onto "something" supernatural and trying to channel it toward you.

"Hidden History of the Human Race" is definitely a monumental album for modern metal, in my opinion. It's certainly one to put in the history books!

BÃ¶lzer are a Swiss death metal band that really made a mark on the "scene" around the mid-2010's. I think that they're a death metal band, but perhaps it's more accurate to say "extreme metal". Einherjer is a Viking metal band from Haugesund, Norway, founded in 1993. Their lyrics retell Norse legends, and each of their albums has its own theme. They use a mixture of both harsh and clean vocals - albums stick to one vocal style.

After listening to their harsh vocals, I started finding a few black metal bands here and there - particularly "Immortal" - which I eventually started consuming voraciously.

Enslaved is a black/progressive metal band from Bergen, Norway. They started with the other 2nd wave of black metal bands in Norway, like Immortal and Burzum. They didn't get involved in the satanic side of that scene, but seemed to kind of do their own thing and focus on more viking themes. They are a very atmospheric band, which contributes to their "progressiveness" that they have these days. That certainly came out in their early, raw music. They focused on the heritage and viking side of things, which I like.

Eventually they moved into a more blatantly progressive direction. In fact, starting with their first EP, it seems like each album became slightly more progressive - meaning less aggressive and more atmospheric and calm. It took a few albums to get there, but I think that's the case. They remain very black metaly into the 2020's, but I think that the album Isa or Ruun in the mid-2000's marked a fairly distinct difference compared to the black metal emphasis.

I personally prefer their earlier music, particularly their first EP, "Hordanes Land". They clearly played long, atmospheric, and progressive music compared to other bands in the early 90's, but this EP also had the incredible and raw aggression to it! I love this EP. Their subsequent album, Vikingligr Veldi is definitely solid, and the follow-up album to that, Frost, is one of my favorites.

I think that there is substantial evidence to prove that Iron Maiden are the greatest band ever. Let me give a brief synopsis, then I'll explain why. Iron Maiden started as a New Wave of British Heavy Metal (NWOBHM) band with their debut album Iron Maiden in 1980. They've been going strong since, releasing 17 albums and continuing to tour in their 60's. Over their career, they've transitioned into more of a progressive rock/metal band.

I think that they're the greatest band for a few simple reasons:

  • Longevity - they started back in the mid-late 70's and are still touring and releasing music, 40+ years later.
  • Influence - countless, countless bands of many rock and metal genres cite Iron Maiden as an influence.
  • Iconic - they're galloping riffs, soaring legato solos, matching vocals, and even imagery (Eddie) make them iconic.
  • Success - at this point in time, they've sold countless albums, travel around the world on an Iron Maiden jet, and even have a whole line of various beers. I'm not talking about them being rich, but rather they're not just musicians. They're businessmen who have built a brand on top of their music.
  • Member consistency - Steve Harris (bassist) is a founding member of the band, and is still the main driver. Dave Murray (guitarist) joined back in the 70's, and while not considered the founder, has essentially been with the band from the beginning. Nicko (drummer) has stayed with the band since '83, and Janick Gers (guitarist) has been with the band since 1990. Bruce (singer) and Adrian (guitar) were part of the "glory days" in the 80's who helped build the brand, both took a 10~ year hiatus during the 90's, then came back and have stayed with the band ever since. There are only 4 former members of Iron Maiden over 40 years, Dennis Stratton (guitarist), Paul Di'Anno (vocals), Blaze Bayley (vocals), and Clive Burr (drummer). It's unbelievably rare for a band to have such dedication in its members, especially over so many decades!

My personal story:
Iron Maiden are kind of considered to be my favorite band. I don't listen to them much anymore - my taste has grown so I listen to a lot of extreme metal. However, something about Maiden captured me when I was younger. The first song I heard of theirs was "Two Minutes to Midnight" back in 9th grade. I liked it, but I didn't really have money or means to acquire my own music - my friend showed me the song on his MP3 player (back when MP3 players were first invented). I kind of liked it, but wasn't infatuated yet. I eventually received a burned CD of their greatest hits. I remember hearing "Rainmaker" and thinking that they were too heavy for me lol. That's one of their only songs with double-bass drumming. What captured me was the vocals, their interesting melodies, and their energy.

Jinjer is a type of djenty, progressivey, metalcorey band from Ukraine. They started back in 2008 and were very hardcore-esque. As time moved on, they've moved in a more progressive and death metaly direction. They have a female frontwoman, Tatiana Shmailyuk, who sings clean and growls. Her growls are very impressive and ferocious, despite no formal training.

Jinjer really exploded in the late 2010's. Around that time, many YouTube creators were making "reaction" videos. Usually, the creator would be a classical opera singer, a music composer, or professional musician of some kind. They would watch videos and give their real-time reaction. Well... I personally think that most were "real-time" reactions (they seem obviously contrived and planned reactions). Anyway, TONS of these videos were created for Jinjer's song "Pisces". Jinjer released a live play-through video that had very artistic aesthetics, and the introduction is calm and gentle. Then, she starts the harsh vocals, and every creator lost their minds. These types of videos really exploded Jinjer.

The reaction videos may have helped Jinjer, but they are not only a result of YouTube hype. Their music is very good as well, Tatiana's clean voice is quite pleasing, and her growls are monstrous. On top of that, the instrumentation is excellent, as well. They started as a hardcore band, which is fairly "dumb guy" type of music, but they always had a little touch of intricate "something". They were growing and gaining notoriety on their own, but the YouTube phenomenon definitely helped maximize the explosion, so to speak.

I definitely like their later music. Their earlier music was very core-y, which is okay, but the later stuff that's more death metaly and progressive is very nice. One of my favorite formats to listen to is the official recording of their 2018 performance at Resurrection Fest (linked below). They played the earlier "core-y" songs, but since they were live, they had an extra "oomph" of aggression and heaviness, which I very much like!

For me personally, I think that I was drawn to Jinjer because of that live performance, which I saw first, but also the juxtaposition between the harsh and clean vocals. Since I got into Opeth, that's become a very attractive vocal style for me. Her growls are quite impressive, despite being a small woman.

Nile are legends. The main guy, Karl Sanders (guitarist), I believe was a history professor. Eventually, he quit that to do the band full time. Nile's themes, if you can't guess from the name, are centered around Egypt - the history, the supernatural, and even the region. Karl started Nile back in the 90's, and the albums have simply gotten better and better over time! A few days ago, my buddy asked for my recommendations of the 'most brutal death metal out there'. I went straight to Cannibal Corpse and Nile.

I think that the main trait of Nile is mixing technical speed with brutality. The guitar work is technical, but also deep and brutal. The drumming - by George Kolias (from Greece) - is unprecedentedly fast with brutal double bass

When I initially started listening to Nile, I was a bit torn. The albums I happened to find were their earlier/intermediate works (let's call them). They clearly had their iconic speed and sound, but they also mixed in the ultra heavy parts, where the tempo slows down and drags on, even though the drums or tremolo picking may be lightning fast. It's peculiar how that can happen - tons of notes are blazing by, but the composition feels really heavy and dragging. I didn't like the dragging parts, but I tolerated them because of the other faster, "headbang-able" parts. I've listened to them for many years, so I retain that stance, but I perhaps they've expanded their discography such that I can listen to the "non-dragging" albums enough to not get bothered.

Anyway, I remember when I was a carpet installer in my early 20's. This is when I found Nile and became obsessed with the album "Annihilation of the Wicked", particularly the title track. I played that track on repeat hundreds of times, it was amazing! The main riff with the melodic bends with the galloping behind it and headbangable doulbe bass all combined together is perfect!

One last note: Karl Sanders also does backing vocals. The primary vocalist always has some of the best death metal vocals, but the fact that Karl adds in the droning gutterals adds some nice variety. His vocals aren't amazing or anything, but I think the fact that it adds a small amount of variation is really nice.

Vastum is a death metal band from San Francisco. They have an old school death metal vibe, mixed with a "cavernous" feeling. Their sound reminds me of a monster in the caverns - they have an echo-y element to their sound and vocals that make it sound like they're blasting something evil from deep inside the earth, or something. They're a death metal band, but they keep their tempos a bit slower (not "doom" slow, though), which makes them very groovy and headbangable.

I found out that they have a main vocalist who does the low gutterals, but their female guitarist Leila also contributes to higher-end vocals, as well. I remember learning that and was very impressed! My taste in death growls, for a long time, definitely catered towards the deeper, heavier voice that males usually create. Her gutterals are higher-pitched than the main vocalist, but they didn't put me off - they are still fairly low and complement the deeper gutterals very well! I've grown to like more variety in my vocals over the years, so I very much like and appreciate this small bit of variety that she brings.

I randomly found their split EP with Spectral Voice on YouTube in the late 2010's. Their old school and groovy vibe hit me immediately, it was incredible! I've always liked the headbangable metal; music with a more definitive and human tempo than some black metal, thrash, and death metal. I feel like it's not just mindless headbanging as fast as you can, but that there's a bit more human feeling to the music since humans can more comfortably move with the music. It's perhaps ironic that I say that since I described them as sounding like a cavernous monster lol. Either way, they're exceptional!

After finding Vastum, that put me in the path towards finding Blood Incantation and eventually Archspire; it also started my trajectory towards a definitive preference for technical death metal.
